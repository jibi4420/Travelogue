Pathiramanal Island, located in the Alappuzha district of Kerala, is a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts. Spanning 10 acres in the serene Vembanad Lake, this green island is a sanctuary for hundreds of rare migratory birds from around the world, as well as a diverse range of flora and fauna. The name "Pathiramanal" translates to "Sands of Night," adding to the mystical allure of this beautiful spot.The island is home to around 90 species of birds, 30 species of butterflies, 160 species of plants, 55 species of fish, and 20 species of spiders.In recent years, the Government of Kerala has initiated the Pathiramanal Biodiversity Conservation for eco-tourism.
